About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate and Graduate Teaching Assistant in Atmospheric Science at the University of Wyoming. My work focuses on orographic precipitation and cold-season convection, using convection-permitting modeling (WRF/MPAS), observational analysis, and satellite-informed evaluation (GPM, CloudSat). I collaborate with scientists at NCAR and run high-resolution experiments on CISL supercomputers to study storm organization, precipitation efficiency, and flood risk in complex terrain.
